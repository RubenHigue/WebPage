Basic studies

I started my scholar studies at Jaén, in the Attendis schools group, where I spend my first six years of education path, at this place I started to develop myself as a student, it was a bilingual school, so I had to study half of my matters, as Natural Science or Maths in English, which is not my native language. Due to this fact, I started getting my Cambridge Certificates at the age of 6 when I got my Movers Certificate. I am grateful that my first educational years were here because it made me realize that the school level must not be only measured by the knowledge you get from it.

When I got to college, I moved from the Attendis school to Maristas Jaén school and stayed here for the next six years of my life. Here I kept on with my educational path but not only as a student but as a person. At Maristas school they gave us what it is called as the Maristas's ethos, where were not only taught on how to pass exams and acquire knowledge but also on how to respect everyone, how to be grateful for everything that happens to us, and how to treat people right and have good manners, alongside with much more other ways to behave in different situations and with different people. At the same time, they gave us a perfect classic education as it has been shown year after year by all the students who had gone through the school. Here I decided that my academic path to follow was going to be the computer science one as so many teachers cheer me up to make it happen. So I will always be thankful for my years here as they not only gave me the courage to face my future career, but also they build me as the person I am right now and gave me the guidelines to follow for my personal development.

University studies

In 2020, I started my degree in Computer Science at the University of Jaén. It was an awkward situation as we entered this new chapter of life with the COVID-19 crisis, so it was very strange not to see many people in class or even don't know your classmates because of this illness, so it was such a situation to deal with. But as this first year passed through and the COVID situation too, I started feeling great as I met a lot of new friends and mates that made my life much easier than this first year. From this first year until this fifth year I have developed myself, making things I thought I was not capable of, and getting out from situations I never imagine I could get out from being successful. As long as the years had been passing by, I am more and more interested in my field of studies, and I am really looking forward to get to my master studies and even my PhD.

Code ORG collaborator

I have been taking part of the Hour of Code project since 2021, my second year of degree, at my college, Maristas Jaén, there I have been teaching kids from the age of 12 to 18 on how to deal with computer science problems and how to code them.
